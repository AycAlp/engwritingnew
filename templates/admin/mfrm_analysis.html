{# ── Interpretation guide ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Reading the MFRM Report
Rater Severity (D measure)

Expressed in logits (log-odds units). The scale is centred so the mean rater severity = 0.

  • Positive logit — rater gives fewer points than average for the same essay (harsher). Example: D = +0.8 → roughly 10–15% lower scores than a calibrated rater.
  • Negative logit — rater gives more points (more lenient).
  • Near zero — rater is well calibrated with the group.
Infit / Outfit MSQ
  • 0.5–1.3 — acceptable. Rater behaves as the model predicts.
  • >1.3 (marginal), >1.5 (underfit) — rater is erratic; scores are less predictable than expected. May need calibration discussion.
  • <0.5 (overfit) — rater scores are suspiciously consistent; may be copying another rater's scores.
Essay Ability (B measure)
  • Higher logit — essay is higher quality and consistently scored higher.
  • Lower logit — essay is weaker and scored lower across raters.
  • High infit MSQ — raters disagreed more than expected on this essay. It may have unusual features worth discussing in calibration.
Fair Score

The score an essay would receive from a rater of average severity. It removes the accident of which rater happened to score which essay, making scores more comparable across the group.

Separation & Reliability
  • Rater separation G ≥ 2 — the instrument can detect at least 3 distinct severity groups among raters. Worth investigating in calibration.
  • Rater reliability ≥ 0.80 — severity estimates are stable and replicable.
  • Essay separation G ≥ 2 — essays represent at least 3 distinct quality levels (good for a representative sample set).
